I am a soil scientist at the Bangladesh Rice Research Institute, where I have worked since 2007 on one of agriculture's hardest climate problems: cutting greenhouse-gas emissions from rice without sacrificing the yields that feed millions.

My PhD examined how urea fertilizer placement and water management shape rice yield, nitrogen-use efficiency, and nitrous-oxide emissions — work carried out under the USAID-funded Feed the Future fertilizer deep-placement program.

Over 19 years I have built expertise across soil fertility, carbon and nitrogen dynamics, soil-salinity management, climate-smart agriculture, and the monitoring and mitigation of greenhouse gases in rice-based cropping systems.

02 — Research Focus

Areas of Expertise

GHG mitigation through soil amendments and water management, climate-resilient fertilizer strategies, soil fertility and plant nutrition, carbon sequestration, and precision agriculture.

🌾

GHG Mitigation in Rice

Measuring and reducing methane and nitrous-oxide emissions from rice-based cropping systems using field-scale experiments and emission modeling.

💧

Water Management

Alternate wetting and drying (AWD) irrigation to cut global warming potential by up to 36% while conserving water without yield penalty.

🧪

Fertilizer Efficiency

Urea deep placement and integrated nutrient management for higher nitrogen-use efficiency and reduced greenhouse gas emissions in saline and non-saline soils.

🧂

Salinity Management

Restoring productivity in coastal saline soils of Bangladesh through climate-resilient agronomic practices and cultivar selection.

🌍

Carbon & Nitrogen Dynamics

Soil carbon sequestration, emission-factor development, and national inventory reporting for UNFCCC climate transparency frameworks.

🛰️

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Scaling low-carbon rice technologies and MRV (monitoring, reporting, verification) systems across agroecological zones of Bangladesh.
